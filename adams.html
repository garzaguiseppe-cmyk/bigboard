PROLOGUE · THE INSTRUMENTS SEE HIM FIRST

2013-14. A rookie averaging three points a game.

That season, four researchers taught a computer to watch pick-and-rolls. Using SportVU tracking they classified 270,823 ball screens — every one in the league — and scored how each defense handled them.

The two stingiest screen-defending pairs in the NBA that year were Fisher-and-Adams and Jackson-and-Adams: 0.00 points per possession allowed when they went "over" the screen. n=14 and n=20 — tiny samples, stated honestly. Their Figure 8 put him more than two standard deviations better than average.

"Steven Adams is a standout defender; regardless of who he is paired with, Adams and his teammate are particularly effective at blocking the defense from scoring off of a pick and roll." McIntyre, Brooks, Guttag & Wiens — SSAC 2016

The instruments could see him before the box score could. The rest of this essay is about why — and about the country that moved him here.

THE TURN

Where does a player like this come from?

Not a highlight machine. A labourer. To understand the game you have to leave it — and go to Rotorua.

ROTORUA · 1993

Size as inheritance.

Born 20 July 1993, in Rotorua — the town he jokes smells of sulphur. Father: Sid Adams, an Englishman and ex–Royal Navy. Mother: Lilika Ngauamo, Tongan, a nurse.

The family was already a legend before Steven — enormous, and so large that even the family disputed the exact number of children. Athletic range ran through it; his half-sister is Dame Valerie Adams, two-time Olympic shot-put champion.

Sources: NBA.com bio; accounts of My Life, My Fight (with M. Chapman); published profiles.

LOSS

He was thirteen.

His mother died in 2000. His father died of stomach cancer in 2007.

He stopped going to school.

Source: accounts of My Life, My Fight.

A brief adolescent drift followed. Secondary material only — accounts of the autobiography; not the headline of his life.

THE DOOR

A person and a rule. No law.

His brother Warren, a former NZ national-team player, phoned Kenny McFadden — the American ex–Washington State guard who had made Wellington home. McFadden arranged a scholarship to Scots College. Blossom Cameron gave him a home in Wellington.

"School every day if you want to play."McFadden's condition — the whole story · ESPN (Royce Young, 2016)

No documented state care, no named welfare programme, no single statute made Steven Adams. The record is personal and institutional: bereavement → drift → Cameron → McFadden's rule → Scots. That is the story; it needs no legislative hook.

SCOTS COLLEGE · WELLINGTON

2.11 metres among the ties.

A grieving 6'11" kid lands in a private-school blazer culture. He arrived unkempt, had never worn a tie; classmates joked "Who's this murderer?"

THE MACHINE · THE FIRST XV ARMS RACE

The scholarship is an instrument.

Behind the blazer sits an economy: school prestige, enrolment marketing, old-boy networks, Sky-broadcast schoolboy rugby — and the scholarship as the device that converts a teenager's body into institutional status. Described coldly, the canonical case is on the public record.

"The bad underbelly of secondary school sport."A principal, on the recruitment culture · NZ Herald (2018)

Still live: in 2025, co-ed principals called a proposed boys-only competition "deeply unethical" (RNZ).

THE LEDGER · THE SPINOFF, 2018

"Hostage to your own talent."

New Zealand journalism has already made the moral argument — so cite it, don't reinvent it. James Harris reported that the majority of recruits are "of Māori or Pasifika descent, and from low income households", nicknamed "imports", "being sold a dream", while "the schools are benefitting from these scholarship programmes more than the students."

And the reveal: Adams rode the American version of the same pipeline next. Notre Dame Prep, Fitchburg MA → one year at Pitt: 32 starts, 7.2 / 6.3 / 2.0, Big East All-Rookie → #12 pick, 2013. He didn't pass through one version of this machine. He passed through both.

Sources: The Spinoff (Harris, 2018); Sports-Reference; NBA.com bio. this is the part the box score can't see

CONTEXT · NOT CAUSE

The world his family belonged to.

Postwar Pasifika labour migration was encouraged for labour needs; Pacific communities were then disproportionately targeted in the 1970s "dawn raids", for which the government later formally apologised.

His Tongan-rooted family belongs to a political story much larger than sport. That is the whole claim of this station — context, not cause. Nothing here made him.

Source: public record; RNZ. Stats NZ 2023 census figures — ⚠ PENDING pull

THE COMPLICATION · WHOSE HANDS

Adams is the door, not the poster victim.

Write it as a finding, not a slogan. On the record: his family initiated; a Black American community coach arranged it; the school said yes; the development work happened substantially at McFadden's 6 a.m. and afternoon sessions for years. What the record shows about "developing in their own community": the community coach was the development — the school provided enrolment, eligibility, literacy, structure.

"What happened to me at Scots is they lit the spark for me."Steven Adams · ESPN (Royce Young, 2016)

The thesis, sharpened: the scholarship is an instrument, and what it does depends on whose hands it's in. In St Kentigern's hands in 2018, the record shows it serving the trophy cabinet. In McFadden's hands, it served the kid.

THE RETURN · 2014

The imported kid becomes the funder.

Within a year of reaching the NBA, Adams funded a scholarship at Scots College himself — reported up to ~NZ$240,000 — for a basketball player showing "determination, humility, and commitment," with McFadden helping select the recipient.

A piece that omits his gratitude and his cheque isn't brave, it's wrong. The counter-ledger stays at equal volume. ⚠ PENDING — verify current status on Scots' site

THE TURN BACK · TO BASKETBALL

What does loss-then-structure build?

Discipline, force, timing, role acceptance. A game of mass and leverage, not authorship. The thesis, stated plainly:

Steven Adams is an elite practitioner of basketball labor — value that arrives before the possession looks beautiful. Three acts follow: he creates possessions, he improves them, he changes the other team's. Then the invoice.

ACT 1 · HE CREATES POSSESSIONS  (REBOUNDING)

The box score undercounts his signature act by more than half.

He led the NBA in offensive rebounds in 2021-22 and 2022-23. A deep-reinforcement-learning valuation put the worth of an offensive rebound at +2.31 expected points — against the traditional +1.0 weight, and up to +3.9 at maximum leverage.

Present as that paper's estimate, not consensus (Jenkins, SSAC 2026). But the direction is the whole point: the single act Adams is best at is the one the traditional ledger prices lowest.

Source: Jenkins — 'Deep Reinforcement Learning for NBA Player Valuation', SSAC 2026; NBA.com bio.

ACT 1 · WHERE MISSES GO

Not all rebounds are the same rebound.

Second-chance possessions run about 1.102 points per possession. And a miss's recoverability depends on where it came from: close-range misses are recovered 38.7% of the time versus 21.0% for mid-range — Adams's habitat is the recoverable zone.

The "team rebounder" problem (the paper's Brook Lopez Effect): a box-out's value often lands in a teammate's rebound and the NBA's deferred rebound chances stat — value created by Adams, collected by someone else.

Source: Stephanos — 'Enhancing NBA True Shot Charts', SSAC 2025. the paper states a 10-game sample — small, noted

ACT 1 · A PROMISE FROM 2012, CASHED IN

Rebounding is a positioning game, not a reach game.

Thirteen years earlier, the same gradient: offensive-rebound rate 36% / 21.5% / 25.5% by miss zone. That paper showed rebounds are secured below 8 feet, with 97.5% of opportunities within ~14 ft of the rim, and named "the value of boxing out" as future work.

ACT 2 · HE IMPROVES POSSESSIONS  (SCREENS)

Six feet is the whole difference.

Across 19,386 three-pointers: an open shot (nearest defender ≥6 ft) went in about 40% of the time; a pressured one about 32%; a defender within six feet even 0.6s before the release dropped it to ~27%.

Screens manufacture those six feet. That is the mechanism — the value is real even when Adams never touches the ball again.

Source: Lucey, Bialkowski, Carr, Yue & Matthews — 'How to Get an Open Shot', SSAC 2014.

ACT 2 · (PASSING)

Connective, not heliocentric.

Movement's value isn't its raw total — it's unpredictability. An entropy measure of ball movement correlates with future open looks (D'Amour, Cervone, Bornn & Goldsberry, 2015); Adams's handoff-hub role is a decision-forcing entropy machine. And pass characteristics matter only at touch times under ~2 seconds — quick-touch passing (Lee) is exactly the shape of his handoff and short-roll assists.

ACT 3 · HE CHANGES THE OTHER TEAM'S POSSESSIONS  (DETERRENCE)

Invisible blocks.

Defender proximity causes lower make probability, strongest at the rim (Tenan & Rezai, causal tracking, 2023). Interior defense suppresses efficiency: league ~49.7% close-range with a big within 5 ft, falling to ~38% against a Hibbert or a Sanders.

Source: Goldsberry & Weiss — 'The Dwight Effect', SSAC 2013. baselines date-stamped 2011–13

ACT 3 · FREQUENCY SUPPRESSION

He changes behaviour, not just shot results.

The other half of interior defense: it moves shots away from the rim entirely. League-wide 57.2% of attempts came at the rim → 48.2% with Howard protecting it. The shots that never get taken are the invisible ones.

ACT 4 · THE INVOICE

A contract is invested capital. Missed games are defaults.

Read honestly (Lautier, SSAC 2025): a deal returns per-game cash flows, and durability is part of the price of labour — Adams missed the entire 2023-24 season (knee). Current terms: a 3-year / $39M extension, ~$13M average annual value (per Spotrac), against a 5.8 / 8.6 / 1.5 line (2025-26).

The rhyme that makes two braids one essay.

neither shows up in the box score.

The whole piece is about learning to see what standard instruments miss — in basketball, and in the country.

METHOD & SOURCES

How to read the numbers.
