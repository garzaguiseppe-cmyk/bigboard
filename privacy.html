Privacy

Guiseppe Drafts
Effective 18 July 2026  ·  Last updated 18 July 2026

Guiseppe Drafts uses optional Google Analytics to understand how the site is used. Analytics is turned off until you accept it, and you can reject it or withdraw at any time. No advertising features, Google Signals or personalised advertising are used. The site's mailto contact form does not transmit anything to the website. GitHub Pages and, if you email the author, your email provider may separately process limited technical or email information as described below.

Analytics — optional, off until you accept

Guiseppe Drafts uses Google Analytics 4 (measurement ID G-0TX5VKF69K), a service operated by Google. It is disabled by default. On your first visit a small banner asks whether you accept analytics. Nothing is loaded from Google, no analytics request is made, and no analytics cookies are created unless and until you choose Accept analytics.

If you accept, Google Analytics is loaded and used to measure, in aggregate: page views and general traffic, which boards are most popular, approximate location (derived by Google from IP address, not stored by this site), device and browser categories, and on-page interactions. This helps the author understand which draft boards people actually read.

What is not used: no advertising features, no Google Ads, no Google Signals, no advertising personalisation, and no user-provided data. Using Google Consent Mode v2, the advertising categories (ad_storage, ad_user_data, ad_personalization) stay denied at all times; only analytics_storage is ever enabled, and only after you accept.

Cookies: the site sets no cookies before consent. After you accept, Google Analytics may set a first-party _ga cookie (and related _ga_* cookies) in your browser to tell visits apart. These are the only cookies the site uses.

Where your data goes: Google receives analytics data only after you accept. For details, see Google's Privacy Policy and how Google Analytics collects and uses data.

Your choice, any time: decline with Reject, or change your mind later using the Analytics preferences link in the footer of every page. Withdrawing consent stops further analytics collection and deletes the Google Analytics cookies that can be removed from the site.

The contact form

The "Send a message" form on the Contact page does not send anything to this website or to any form-processing service. When you press Send, your browser opens your own email application with the message pre-filled and addressed to garzaguiseppe@gmail.com.

  • What it collects: the name, email and message you type are used only to build that pre-filled email in your own email app.
  • Who processes it: nothing is transmitted to or stored by this site. If you choose to send the email, it is delivered like any email you send — through your own email provider into the garzaguiseppe@gmail.com inbox, a Gmail account operated by Guiseppe Garza and hosted by Google.
  • Why: solely so you can contact the site's author.

You can also reach the author directly by email or via the linked social accounts. Those platforms operate under their own privacy policies.

Hosting and server logs

The site is hosted on GitHub Pages. Like most web hosts, GitHub may automatically record standard technical request information (such as IP address and browser details) in server logs as part of delivering the site. That processing is carried out by GitHub under its own privacy practices, not by Guiseppe Drafts.

Retention

Google Analytics event data for this property is retained for 2 months, after which Google automatically deletes it; the “reset on new activity” option is turned off, so the retention period is not extended when a visitor returns. The site itself stores no personal data on its own servers. Any email you choose to send to garzaguiseppe@gmail.com is kept in that inbox at the author's discretion.

Access, correction and deletion

To stop analytics and remove analytics cookies at any time, use the Analytics preferences link in the footer and choose Reject. For analytics data already collected, Google acts as the data processor; you can ask the author, or use Google's own controls. If you have emailed garzaguiseppe@gmail.com and would like your message deleted, email that address to ask.
